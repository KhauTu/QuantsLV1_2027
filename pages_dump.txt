Let’s detail the characteristics across major financial assets:
Cash:does not generate any return unless invested. In the short term, excess cash is typically placed in
interest-bearing bank accounts or short-term debt instruments.
Equities:generate returns from capital appreciation and discretionary dividends (variable capital distri-
butions). Rapidly growing companies often choose not to distribute dividends and instead reinvest earnings
to fuel growth.
Bonds (Debt):represent contractual lending agreements that pay fixed interest coupons (fixed capital
distributions). Shorter-term bonds are dominated by capital distributions, whereas longer-term bonds
exhibit substantial price volatility due to market interest rate movements.
Hybrids:preferred stock and convertible bonds blend fixed-income features of debt with the growth
potential of equity.
2.1.2 Types of Returns: Financial Instruments
Financial instruments are standardized legal agreements for financial assets (such as equity and bonds), as well
as derivatives and securitized debt. The standardization facilitates public trading.
Cash Instruments:represent direct ownership claims on financial assets (e.g., actual shares of stock or
actual corporate bonds).
Derivatives:financial instruments whose value is derived from the performance of another underlying
financial asset or indicator (e.g., stock price, interest rate, index, exchange rate). A call option is a typical
example.
2.1.3 Types of Returns: Financial Indicators
Financial indicators do not directly generate cash flows, but are critical to financial market operations.
Interest Rates:traded through risk-free government bonds. Their level varies inversely with bond prices.
The Risk-F ree Rate:represented by short-term, risk-free government debt (e.g., US Treasury bills).
Exam Tip
Short-term discount instruments (like T-bills) do not pay regular coupons (Inct = 0). Instead, they sell
at a discount to face value, and the return is earned purely from the price appreciation when the bond
matures at par value.
